I am a Ph. D. candidate in computer science at the University of Minnesota (UMN), Twin Cities. I work at the Interactive Robotics and Vision Lab (IRVLab) and supervised by Prof. Junaed Sattar. My research interests lie in robot vision and deep learning, particularly in the design and development of robust deep visual modules for near real-time use by underwater robots. I also explore research problems in the domains of applied machine learning and 3d computer vision. Check out some of my recent projects and the list of publications; feel free to reach out and be connected.


About Me

I am from Bangladesh, a small and beautiful country in South Asia. I pursued my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees at Bangladesh University of Eng. and Tech. (BUET) in computer science and engineering (CSE) in the year 2012 and 2015, respectively. I also worked as a Lecturer at the dept. of CSE, United Int. University (UIU) during 2012-15. Then I moved to Minneapolis, the city of lakes and started my Ph.D. marathon at the UMN in Fall 2015.

Outside work, I play and follow cricket, the gentleman's game like a nerd. I also love traveling and taking pictures; checkout my leisure pleasures and lets talk if we have common interests!
